Anti-Money Laundering Policy

Last Updated: January 2025

Policy Statement

Harrington Capital is committed to preventing money laundering and terrorist financing. We maintain a comprehensive Anti-Money Laundering (AML) compliance program in accordance with the Bank Secrecy Act, USA PATRIOT Act, and all applicable federal and state regulations.

Customer Identification Program (CIP)

We verify the identity of all clients through our Customer Identification Program, which includes:

  • Collection of identifying information (name, address, tax ID, date of incorporation)
  • Verification of information through documentary and non-documentary methods
  • Beneficial ownership identification for business entities
  • Enhanced due diligence for high-risk clients

Transaction Monitoring

We monitor all transactions for suspicious activity and unusual patterns. Our monitoring includes review of transaction size, frequency, origin, and business purpose.

Suspicious Activity Reporting

We file Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) with FinCEN when required by law. All employees are trained to identify and report potentially suspicious activity.

Record Retention

We maintain records of all client identification information, transaction records, and AML compliance activities in accordance with federal requirements (minimum 5 years).

AML Compliance Officer

Our designated AML Compliance Officer oversees all aspects of our AML program, including policy implementation, training, testing, and regulatory reporting.
